In this lab, I built and trained both UNet and ResNet34_UNet models from scratch for the pet image binary segmentation task . UNet works by zooming out step by step to find features, then zooming in while combining earlier details to draw sharp boundaries . ResNet34_UNet simply uses ResNet34 to extract high-level features first, then gradually rebuilds the prediction map . When comparing them, ResNet34_UNet learns much faster early on and gets more stable scores because of its residual connections, while UNet takes longer to learn but is great at catching fine border details as long as the image shape is not distorted. For data preprocessing, I classified the pixels based on whether the mask equals 1 or the mask does not equal 1 . To prevent UNet images from stretching, I zoomed them in or out proportionally and added black borders, then cut those borders out during inference. For ResNet34_UNet, I simply resized everything to 256 by 256. Both models used Albumentations to create more training data. For training, I used AdamW with a scheduler, and used the dice score and loss to adjust and learn . I asked AI how to improve the Dice score, and in the inference stage, I asked AI to write the code that converts my prediction results into the encoding format required by Kaggle, as well as how to merge images of different sizes using center crop.
